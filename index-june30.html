01

Machine Learning & Deployment

BC Wildfire Danger Prediction System

Machine Learning · Cloud Data Pipeline · AWS

Featured · Live

An end-to-end ML system that predicts daily wildfire danger across British Columbia. It ingests weather and satellite data from multiple sources, runs a trained model over a province-wide grid, and automatically refreshes a live public map every day with no manual intervention. Built with rigorous temporal validation, input-distribution monitoring to catch data drift, and an honest public disclosure of the model's accuracy and limitations.

About & Contact

I'm a Data Science student at Simon Fraser University drawn to the parts of the job that go beyond fitting a model: making a pipeline reproducible, validating inputs before they reach production, monitoring outputs for drift, and explaining results clearly to people who aren't data scientists. I care about building things that are trustworthy and honest, and about the engineering that keeps them running on their own. Above all, I believe data has the most value when it is communicated clearly and ethically, giving people what they need to make informed decisions that align with their own values.
